Apollo, God of the Sun
DIVINE
God
Art Prompt:
Tech noir cyberpunk masterpiece TCG art, Apollo as a solar-powered digital deity with fiber-optic hair radiating light. Holographic lyre and burning solar panels. Chariot transformed into light-speed vehicle. Cyber-sunrise over neon cityscape.
Divine Power
Monsters destroyed by battle this turn are banished instead.
Mythological Origins
Apollo, the radiant god of music, prophecy, and the sun, was one of the most widely worshipped deities across Greek and Roman cultures. His oracle at Delphi was consulted by rulers throughout the ancient world, and his healing temples served as early hospitals from Greece to Gaul. He commanded prophetic sight, could heal or bring plague with his silver bow, drove the sun across the sky in his golden chariot, and his musical abilities could charm gods and mortals alike.
Ares, God of War
DIVINE
God
Art Prompt:
Tech noir cyberpunk masterpiece TCG art, Ares as a cyber-warrior in burning battle-armor with digital weapons. Explosive plasma blasts and burning battlefield wreckage. War-drones and automated weapons systems. Devastated cyberpunk warzone with smoking ruins.
Divine Power
Once per turn, if a monster you control destroys an opponent's monster by battle: That monster can attack again.
Mythological Origins
Ares embodied the brutal, chaotic aspects of warfare in Greek mythology, feared even by his fellow Olympians for his savage nature. The Romans elevated him as Mars, their patron deity of military might and divine father of Romulus and Remus. He could inspire berserker fury in warriors, command supernatural strength and endurance, summon phantom armies, and his very presence on a battlefield could turn the tide of any conflict.
Zeus, King of Olympus
DIVINE
God
Art Prompt:
Tech noir cyberpunk masterpiece TCG art, Zeus as a digital god-emperor wreathed in electric storms. Crackling cyber-lightning bolts arcing between chrome-plated fingers. Holographic thunderclouds swirling around towering form. Storm-torn digital sky with floating data fragments.
Divine Power

Mythological Origins
Zeus reigns supreme as king of the Olympian gods in Greek mythology, ruling from Mount Olympus with his mighty thunderbolt. Ancient Greeks, Romans (as Jupiter), and cultures across the Mediterranean revered him as the sky father and ultimate divine authority. His legendary battles against the Titans and his role as protector of justice and order made him the most powerful deity in the classical pantheon.

LEGENDARY

Divine Spells & Divinations (Alphabetical)

Aegis Protocol
RARE
Quick-Play Spell
Art Prompt:
Tech noir cyberpunk masterpiece TCG art, a shimmering digital shield forming around a cyber-warrior. Holographic Greek patterns and defensive algorithms. Glowing protective barriers and deflected energy attacks. Ancient wisdom symbols floating in data streams.
Protective Power
Activate when your Monster is attacked. Reduce the battle damage to 0 this turn.
Mystical Origins
The Aegis was the legendary shield of Zeus, later carried by Athena, that could turn enemies to stone and deflect any attack. This digital recreation of the ancient protective power creates an impenetrable barrier around the user, channeling the defensive wisdom of the gods into modern warfare. The protocol activates the moment danger is detected, surrounding the target with layers of divine protection that even the strongest attacks cannot penetrate.
